I’ve finished running the EOF analysis with the data (daily, volume-weighted, inside the eddy). I took the Storm dates from Mousa. The results actually match your hypothesis quite well. Mode 1 basically tracks the total amount of large particles through the whole water column, and PC1 lines up almost perfectly with the storm periods (drop around 7–10 May, then strong spikes around 12–14 and 17–19 May). Mode 2 shows a clear deep–shallow opposite pattern that looks like turbulence-driven disaggregation and upward redistribution, and its PC peaks during the second storm. Mode 3 captures shifts in the size structure rather than bulk concentration, with a smaller response later in the cruise.
I’m still not fully confident about the left EOF maps,  they look a bit different than I expected . But I followed your instructions as closely as possible and tried to make the processing correct. I’ve also attached the draft version of the full code I used, in case you want to take a quick look.
